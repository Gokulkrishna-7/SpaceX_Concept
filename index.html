SPACE X MISSION

The mission of taking humans to space by Elon Musk, through SpaceX, is primarily known as the Commercial Crew Program when partnering with NASA to transport astronauts to the International Space Station (ISS). Another notable mission focused on taking humans to space, including future plans for Mars, is called Starship. The Starship project aims to develop a fully reusable spacecraft capable of carrying humans to the Moon, Mars, and beyond.

TAKING HUMANS TO SPACE

SpaceX's human spaceflight missions aim to make space travel accessible and safe using the Dragon spacecraft and Falcon rockets. Collaborating with NASA, they transport astronauts to the ISS and are pioneering space tourism. Future plans include taking humans to Mars, striving for sustainable colonization. These efforts revolutionize space access and exploration.

VISIT THE BLACK HOLE

A black hole is an astronomical object with a gravitational pull so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. Formed from the remnants of massive stars that have collapsed under their own gravity, black holes are detected by their influence on nearby matter and radiation.

MISSION ONGOING

Event Horizon Telescope (EHT)

An international collaboration aiming to capture images of black hole event horizons, such as the famous image of M87*.

LISA (Laser Interferometer Space Antenna):

A planned space mission to detect and observe gravitational waves from merging black holes.

SPACEX DESIGNS

FALCON 1

Falcon 1 was SpaceX's first orbital launch vehicle, designed to provide reliable and cost-effective access to space for small payloads.

FALCON 9

Falcon 9 is a reusable two-stage rocket developed by SpaceX for the reliable and safe transport of satellites and cargo to orbit and beyond.

FALCON HEAVY

Falcon Heavy is a powerful reusable rocket developed by SpaceX, designed to carry large payloads to orbit and beyond, with three Falcon 9 boosters providing increased lift capacity.

B.F.R

The Big Falcon Rocket (BFR), now known as Starship, is SpaceX's fully reusable spacecraft designed for deep space missions, including Mars colonization.
